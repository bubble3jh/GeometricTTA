# [Agent Task Specification] BATCLIP Diagnostics & Hypothesis Testing

**Experimental Setup:**
* **Model/Dataset:** ViT-B-16, CIFAR-10-C (Target: `severity=5`, isolated to `Noise` corruptions vs non-noise).
* **Optimization:** Batch Size = 200, N = 1000 (Total 5 update steps per domain). Update LayerNorm (`vision+text LN`) only.
* **Prompt:** `"a photo of a <CLS>"`

**Agent Instruction:** Implement the following 6 test groups. Do not propose new methods. Your job is to inject logging, intermediate metric calculations, and controlled interventions into the BATCLIP `forward`/`backward` loops and output the required correlations/AUCs.

---

## Group 1: Causal Decomposition of the Bottleneck
*Determine if the failure is rooted in feature representation, label assignment, or sample size.*

### H8: Representation Collapse vs. Assignment Confusion
* **Task:** At each step $t$, compute two sets of prototypes (Log-only, do not use for update):
    * `v_pseudo`: $\bar{v}_c^{(pseudo)}(t)$ using BATCLIP's pseudo-labels.
    * `v_true`: $\mu_k^{(true)}(t) = \mathbb{E}[v|y=k]$ using Oracle true labels.
    * **Regression check:** Fit `v_pseudo` as a linear combination of `v_true` to see if prototypes are just mixed, or fundamentally distorted.
* **Metrics:** Track $Var_{inter}^{pseudo}(t)$, $Var_{inter}^{true}(t)$, and $Var_{intra}^{true}(t)$.
* **Criteria:**
    * *Accept H8a (Assignment):* $Var_{inter}^{true}$ is stable, but $Var_{inter}^{pseudo}$ collapses. (Key = Robust estimation).
    * *Accept H8b (Representation):* $Var_{inter}^{true}$ collapses. (Key = Feature space preservation).

### H9: Causality of High-Margin Wrong (Oracle Intervention)
* **Task:** Run two separate oracle intervention passes (Modify the update loop):
    * `Oracle-drop`: Remove samples where $(q > 0.5 \text{ AND } \hat{y} \neq y_{true})$ from prototype calculation.
    * `Oracle-correct`: Reassign $(q > 0.5 \text{ AND } \hat{y} \neq y_{true})$ samples to their $y_{true}$ for prototype calculation.
* **Metrics:** $\Delta Var_{inter}(t)$ trajectory and final mCE.
* **Criteria:** If interventions significantly restore $Var_{inter}$ and accuracy $\rightarrow$ Overconfident-wrong is the definitive causal bottleneck.

### H10: N-Dependence (Variance vs. Optimization Dynamics)
* **Task:** Take N=10K data. Split into 10 independent blocks of N=1K.
* **Metrics:** Compare the mean performance of [Bootstrap/Independent 1K blocks] vs. [Sequential online 10K accumulation].
* **Criteria:** If 10K sequentially beats the expected mean of 1K resamples $\rightarrow$ Stability comes from optimization dynamics (accumulated moving averages), not just statistical variance.

## Group 2: Internal Optimization Dynamics
*Understand why the gradient steps lead to collapse.*

### H12: Gradient Conflict
* **Task:** Before optimizer step, compute gradients of each loss term w.r.t the LN parameters: $g_{ent}, g_{pm}, g_{sp}$.
* **Metrics:** Cosine similarities $\cos(g_{ent}, g_{pm})$, $\cos(g_{ent}, g_{sp})$, $\cos(g_{pm}, g_{sp})$.
* **Criteria:** If $\cos < 0$ (conflict) strongly predicts a negative $\Delta Var_{inter}$ in the next step $\rightarrow$ Objective terms are structurally fighting under noise.

### H13: High Leverage of Impure Classes
* **Task:** For each batch, log $n_c$ (pseudo count per class) and the true purity of that pseudo-class.
* **Metrics:** Calculate the contribution magnitude of each class prototype to $L_{pm}$ and $L_{sp}$.
* **Criteria:** If classes with small $n_c$ and low purity dominate the gradient/loss $\rightarrow$ Problem is "high leverage" of minority contaminated samples.

## Group 3: Alternative Confidence Signals (Replacing $\bar{r}$ and $q$)
*Find test-time observable signals to filter the 21-27% high-margin wrongs.*

### H14: Absolute Evidence
* **Task:** Isolate the subset where margin $q > 0.5$.
* **Metrics:** Calculate absolute evidence $s_{max} = \max_c \cos(v, z_c)$ (or max logit/energy). Compute AUC ($s_{max} \rightarrow$ Correct) within this high-margin subset.
* **Criteria:** If AUC > 0.6 $\rightarrow$ Relative confidence ($q$) fails, but absolute confidence holds.

### H15: Augmentation Consistency (Multi-view)
* **Task:** Apply $M$ weak augmentations per image. Calculate agreement $a_i = \frac{1}{M} \sum \mathbb{1}[\hat{y}^{(m)} = \text{mode}]$.
* **Metrics:** Compute AUC ($a_i \rightarrow$ Correct) within the high-margin subset.
* **Criteria:** If AUC is high $\rightarrow$ Overconfident wrongs are unstable across views.

### H16: Neighborhood Agreement (kNN)
* **Task:** Maintain a feature bank of the recent $K$ batches. Calculate $u_i$ = kNN pseudo-label match rate.
* **Metrics:** Compute AUC ($u_i \rightarrow$ Correct) within the high-margin subset.
* **Criteria:** If AUC is high $\rightarrow$ Overconfident wrongs are isolated outliers in feature space.

## Group 4: Systematic Confusion Diagnostics
*Explain why $\bar{r}$ has an inverse correlation (AUC < 0.5) with correctness.*

### H18: The Sink Class (Dominant Confusion)
* **Task:** Calculate the conditional prediction distribution: $P(\hat{y} | \text{wrong}, q > 0.5)$.
* **Metrics:** Entropy/Gini of this distribution.
* **Criteria:** If entropy is highly skewed (concentrated in 1-2 classes) $\rightarrow$ Noise creates a systematic "Black Hole" pull, not random errors.

### H19: Self-Reinforcing Alignment Loop
* **Task:** Track class purity $\pi_c(t)$ and alignment $a_c(t) = \bar{v}_c(t) \cdot z_c(t)$.
* **Metrics:** Correlation between $\pi_c(t)$ and the growth rate $\Delta a_c$.
* **Criteria:** If negative correlation $\rightarrow$ $L_{pm}$ is mistakenly pulling impure visual prototypes closer to the text anchors, reinforcing the error.

---

## Group 5 & 6: Feature Geometry and Early Shock
*Identify where and when the collapse happens in the network.*

### H20: Effective Dimensionality Collapse
* **Task:** Compute batch feature covariance $\Sigma$ and its eigenvalues $\lambda_i$.
* **Metrics:** Effective rank $d_{eff} = (\sum \lambda_i)^2 / \sum \lambda_i^2$.
* **Criteria:** Check correlation between $d_{eff}$, $Var_{inter}$, and Accuracy. If $d_{eff}$ drops sharply $\rightarrow$ Global directional collapse.

### H21: Layer-wise Vulnerability
* **Task:** Log $\|\Delta\gamma_\ell\|$ and $\|\Delta\beta_\ell\|$ per Transformer layer $\ell$.
* **Metrics:** Regress layer update norm against next step's $\Delta Var_{inter}$.
* **Criteria:** Identifies if specific layers (e.g., late visual layers) are disproportionately causing the collapse.

### H22: The Early Shock
* **Task:** Isolate $\Delta Var_{inter}^{early} = Var_{inter}(1) - Var_{inter}(0)$.
* **Metrics:** Correlate this step 1 delta with the final step 5 accuracy.
* **Criteria:** If highly correlated $\rightarrow$ The very first noisy batch permanently damages the model, pointing to the need for conservative initial updates.
